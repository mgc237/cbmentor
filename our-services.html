Saturday School

Founded in partnership with Wolfson College, University of Cambridge, our Saturday School takes place on Saturday mornings during term time. Actually, working as a homework club, students are encouraged to bring their homework or coursework with them and have the support of trained University of Cambridge students who tutor participants. We have seen incredible results with both students and their parents commenting on the improvements in their educational attainment and general attitude towards learning.

Sessions are free to attend, although we do require students to commit to a term at a time and to be registered with CB Mentoring.Â 

Social - Cultural Activities

Our aim is to provide a wealth of experiences which will educate inform or inspire young people. We are pleased to have hosted a range of workshops for children of both primary and secondary school ages. To date, these have been one off workshops, ranging from health and fitness sessions in partnership with Nuffield Health, to a culture and identity arts session at Djibo Arts, Peterborough. We also found a way to teach maths as well as the importance of being responsible in a creative way, thanks to a cocktail making class (non alcoholic) thanks to Hidden Rooms Cambridge.

You can contact us if you would like to partner with CB Mentoring to host an upcoming session.

Mentoring

Mentoring makes such a difference to a young persons life and can be required for many reasons. We provide 1:1 mentoring opportunities with fully trained mentors who are hand picked by our team to make sure they are able to support your child. We can help with educational stressors, family breakdowns, anti-social behaviour, bullying etc so please reach out if you are in need of help and support for a young person in your care.

If you would like to know more and potentially sign someone up as a mentee, contact us.
